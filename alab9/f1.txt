1.In 2005, Nature published a peer review comparing 42 science articles from 
2.Encyclopedia Britannica and Wikipedia and found that Wikipedia's level of 
3.accuracy approached that of Britannica.[16] Time magazine stated that the open-door 
4.policy of allowing anyone to edit had made Wikipedia the biggest and possibly the 
5.best encyclopedia in the world, and was a testament to the vision of Jimy Wales.[17]
6.Wikipedia has been criticized for exhibiting systemic bias, 
7.for presenting a mixture of "truths, half truths, and some falsehoods",
8.and for being subject to manipulation and spin in controversial topics.
9.In 2017, Facebook announced that it would help readers detect fake news
10.by suitable links to Wikipedia articles. YouTube announced a similar plan in 2018. 
11.In response, The Washington Post headlined, "Wikipedia, the 'good cop' of the Internet".[20]
